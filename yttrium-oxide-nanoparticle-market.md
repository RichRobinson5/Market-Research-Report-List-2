They are commonly employed in the production of solid oxide fuel cells, heat-resistant ceramics, and cutting tools, among others. Additionally, yttrium oxide nanoparticles find extensive application in the electronics industry, particularly in the manufacturing of electronics components, optical coatings, and phosphors for display devices.</p><p>In the healthcare sector, yttrium oxide nanoparticles have gained significant attention for their potential therapeutic applications.
