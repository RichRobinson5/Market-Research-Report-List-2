<p><p>Phosphodiesterase Type-5 (PDE5) inhibitors are a class of drugs that block the enzyme phosphodiesterase type-5, which is responsible for the breakdown of cyclic guanosine monophosphate (cGMP). By inhibiting PDE5, these drugs increase the levels of cGMP, leading to smooth muscle relaxation and improved blood flow to certain areas of the body. PDE5 inhibitors are primarily used to treat erectile dysfunction (ED), and the most commonly known drug in this class is sildenafil (Viagra).</p><p>The current outlook of the PDE5 inhibitor market is promising, with steady growth expected in the coming years. Factors such as the increasing prevalence of ED, growing awareness and acceptance of pharmaceutical treatments, and advancements in drug delivery mechanisms are driving the market growth.
