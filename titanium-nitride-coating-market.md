<p><p>Titanium Nitride Coating is applied to various surfaces to enhance their hardness, resistance to wear, and aesthetic appeal. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) is a process where a high temperature is used to decompose titanium tetrachloride and ammonia gases, resulting in the deposition of titanium nitride on the surface. Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) involves the deposition of titanium nitride through the bombardment of titanium atoms on the surface by high-energy particles, creating a thin film with improved properties. Both methods are widely used in different industries for their unique benefits and applications.</p></p>
