V2V (vehicle-to-vehicle) pertains to communication between vehicles, enhancing safety and efficiency through real-time data sharing. V2I (vehicle-to-infrastructure) involves communication between vehicles and infrastructures like traffic lights or road signs, enabling optimized traffic flow and reducing congestion. V2P (vehicle-to-pedestrian) facilitates the exchange of information between vehicles and pedestrians, improving safety by warning drivers of pedestrian presence. "Other" refers to emerging applications like V2N (vehicle-to-network), V2G (vehicle-to-grid), or V2B (vehicle-to-building), enabling interaction with networks, energy systems, or buildings for additional functionality.</p></p>
